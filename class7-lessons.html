Grade 7 Maths Lesson Plan - Large Numbers Around Us

COMPETENCY-BASED LESSON PLAN

Grade: 7 Subject: Maths
Chapter: Large Numbers Around Us Concept: Lakhs, Crores, Operations, Estimation
Total Days: 5 No. of Periods: 6
Date: 15/04/2025 Begins on: 16/04/2025
Resources Required: NCERT Textbook, CBSE Exemplar, Number Charts, Activity Sheets, GeoGebra

Learning Outcomes:

Students will be able to:

  • Read/write numbers in Indian (lakhs, crores) and International systems (millions, billions)
  • Perform operations (+, –, ×) with large numbers using shortcuts
  • Round off and estimate real-life quantities (e.g., population, distances)
  • Solve contextual problems (e.g., "Can 1 lakh buses fit Mumbai's population?")

ACTIVITIES

1. Place Value and Number Systems

  • Indian vs. International System Chart
    • Students create a comparison chart showing how numbers like 1,00,000 (1 lakh) and 1,000,000 (1 million) are written and read in both systems.
    • Materials: Number cards, place value charts.
  • Toothpick Digits
    • Use toothpicks to form digits (e.g., digit "7" requires 3 sticks). Students create the largest/smallest number possible with 24 sticks.
    • Extension: Rearrange sticks to change numbers (e.g., turn "42,019" into "42,078").

2. Operations with Large Numbers

  • Land of Tens Calculator Game
    • Simulate calculators with only "+1000" or "+10" buttons. Students calculate how many presses are needed to reach numbers like 53,000 or 1 lakh.
    • Example: "Press +1000 53 times to get 53,000."
  • Multiplication Shortcuts
    • Solve problems like 25 × 240 by breaking into (1000 ÷ 4) × 240.
    • Worksheet: Include problems like 72 × 125 (using 125 = 1000 ÷ 8).

3. Estimation and Rounding

  • Population Round-Off
    • Given real data (e.g., Chintamani's population: 75,000 vs. 1,06,000), students round to nearest lakh/thousand and justify choices.
    • Discussion: When to round up (e.g., event planning) vs. down (e.g., budgeting).
  • Estimate the Sum
    • Groups estimate sums like 4,63,128 + 4,19,682 and check actual results. Discuss strategies (e.g., rounding to lakhs first).

4. Real-World Applications

  • Can 1 Lakh Buses Fit Mumbai?
    • Calculate: If 1 bus carries 50 people, how many buses needed for Mumbai's 1.24 crore population?
    • Math Talk: "Why might exact numbers matter here?"
  • Distance to the Moon
    • Convert 3,84,400 km into days of travel (e.g., "If you travel 100 km/day, how many years to reach the Moon?").

5. Creative and Collaborative Tasks

  • Large Number Fact Posters
    • Research/create posters on facts like "Amazon River discharges 4,00,00,000 liters/sec" or "Purandaradasa composed 4,75,000 songs."
    • Presentation: Students explain the math behind the facts.
  • Number Name Challenge
    • Write 7-digit number names with maximum letters (e.g., 77,77,777 = "Seventy-seven lakh seventy-seven thousand seven hundred seventy-seven").

Assessment Tasks

  1. Write in words: 27,30,000 (Indian system) vs. 2,730,000 (International)
  2. Estimate: Is 4,63,128 + 4,19,682 closer to 8 or 9 lakhs?
  3. Solve: If Purandaradasa composed 4,75,000 songs in 50 years, songs/year?
  4. Create: Largest number using 24 toothpicks (e.g., 98,711 uses 23 sticks)
