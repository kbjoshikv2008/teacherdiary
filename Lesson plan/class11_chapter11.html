Content and Concepts
विषयवस्तु एवं संकल्पनाएँ
  • Introduction to 3D coordinate system: Extending from 2D to 3D
  • Coordinate axes (X, Y, Z) and coordinate planes (XY, YZ, ZX)
  • Octants: The eight divisions of 3D space
  • Coordinates of a point in space: Ordered triplet (x, y, z)
  • Sign conventions in different octants
  • Distance formula in 3D space: \[ \sqrt{(x_2-x_1)^2 + (y_2-y_1)^2 + (z_2-z_1)^2} \]
  • Applications: Collinearity of points, properties of geometric figures
  • Section formula and centroid (brief introduction)

3D Coordinate System

Coordinate Planes

Key Concept: In 3D space, we extend the 2D coordinate system by adding a third axis (Z-axis) perpendicular to the XY-plane. Any point in space can be represented by an ordered triplet (x, y, z).
Example: Distance Calculation
Find distance between P(1,-3,4) and Q(-4,1,2)
Solution: \[ \sqrt{(-4-1)^2 + (1+3)^2 + (2-4)^2} = \sqrt{25+16+4} = \sqrt{45} = 3\sqrt{5} \]

Learning Outcomes (NCERT)
अधिगम उपलब्धियाँ

After studying this chapter, students will be able to:

  • Understand the extension of coordinate geometry from 2D to 3D space
  • Identify coordinate axes, planes, and octants in 3D space
  • Determine the coordinates of a point in 3D space
  • Apply sign conventions based on octant location
  • Calculate distance between two points in 3D space
  • Verify collinearity of points using distance formula
  • Solve problems involving geometric shapes in 3D space
  • Understand the concept of centroid in 3D
Pedagogical Strategies
शैक्षणिक रणनीतियाँ
  • 3D models: Use physical models to demonstrate coordinate axes and planes
  • Classroom mapping: Designate classroom walls as coordinate planes
  • Technology integration: Use GeoGebra 3D for visualization
  • Real-world examples: Use GPS coordinates, aircraft navigation
  • Group activities: Octant identification exercises
  • Problem-solving sessions: Solve NCERT examples step-by-step
  • Historical context: Discuss contributions of Descartes and Fermat
  • Hands-on activity: Create 3D coordinate systems using strings
