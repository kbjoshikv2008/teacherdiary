Content and Concepts
विषयवस्तु एवं संकल्पनाएँ
  • Introduction to Random Experiments and Sample Spaces
  • Events: Types of events (Simple, Compound, Impossible, Sure)
  • Axiomatic Approach to Probability
  • Probability of Events: P(E) = n(E)/n(S)
  • Probability of Complementary Events: P(E') = 1 - P(E)
  • Mutually Exclusive and Exhaustive Events
  • Independent Events and Multiplication Theorem
  • Conditional Probability: P(A|B) = P(A∩B)/P(B)
  • Baye's Theorem and its Applications
  • Random Variables and Probability Distributions
  • Mean and Variance of Random Variables
Key Concept: Probability measures the likelihood of occurrence of events: 0 ≤ P(E) ≤ 1
Sample Problem: A die is thrown. Find the probability of getting:
(a) A prime number
(b) A number greater than 4
Solution:
Sample space S = {1,2,3,4,5,6}
(a) P(prime) = P{2,3,5} = 3/6 = 1/2
(b) P(>4) = P{5,6} = 2/6 = 1/3
Learning Outcomes (NCERT)
अधिगम उपलब्धियाँ

After studying this chapter, students will be able to:

  • Define random experiments, outcomes, and sample spaces
  • Differentiate between various types of events
  • Apply axiomatic approach to calculate probabilities
  • Compute probabilities of complementary and compound events
  • Determine when events are mutually exclusive or independent
  • Calculate conditional probabilities using appropriate formulas
  • Apply Baye's theorem to solve practical problems
  • Define random variables and construct probability distributions
  • Compute mean and variance of random variables
  • Solve real-world problems using probability concepts
Pedagogical Strategies
शैक्षणिक रणनीतियाँ
  • Experimental learning: Use coin tosses, dice rolls to demonstrate probability concepts
  • Visual aids: Venn diagrams for event relationships and probability calculations
  • Real-world application: Analyze probability in weather forecasting, games, insurance
  • Group activities: Probability experiments with cards, dice, and spinners
  • Technology integration: Use probability simulators and Excel for data analysis
  • Case studies: Medical diagnosis using Baye's theorem
  • Problem-solving sessions: Step-by-step modeling of probability problems
Sample Activity: The Birthday Paradox
Students calculate the probability that two people in a group share the same birthday.
For 23 people: P(at least one shared birthday) ≈ 50.7%
This demonstrates counterintuitive probability concepts.
