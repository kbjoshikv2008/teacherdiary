Content and Concepts
विषयवस्तु एवं संकल्पनाएँ
  • Introduction to Calculus as the mathematics of change
  • Intuitive idea of derivatives (velocity example)
  • Concept of limits and limit notation: \(\lim_{x \to a} f(x)\)
  • Left-hand and right-hand limits
  • Algebra of limits: sum, difference, product, quotient
  • Limits of polynomials and rational functions
  • Important limits:
    • \(\lim_{x \to a} \frac{x^n - a^n}{x - a} = na^{n-1}\)
    • \(\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x} = 1\)
    • \(\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{1 - \cos x}{x} = 0\)
  • Definition of derivative (first principle): \(f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h}\)
  • Algebra of derivatives: sum, difference, product, quotient rules
  • Derivatives of standard functions: \(x^n, \sin x, \cos x, \text{constant}\)
Key Concept: The derivative represents instantaneous rate of change. Geometrically, it's the slope of the tangent to the curve at a point.
Learning Outcomes (NCERT)
अधिगम उपलब्धियाँ

After studying this chapter, students will be able to:

  • Understand the intuitive idea of derivatives as rate of change
  • Define and interpret limits algebraically and graphically
  • Evaluate limits of various functions using limit theorems
  • Compute derivatives using first principles
  • Apply sum, difference, product and quotient rules for differentiation
  • Find derivatives of polynomial and trigonometric functions
  • Solve problems involving instantaneous rates of change
  • Relate derivatives to slopes of tangents and velocities
Pedagogical Strategies
शैक्षणिक रणनीतियाँ
  • Real-world examples: Use falling object experiment to demonstrate instantaneous velocity
  • Visual learning: Graphical representation of limits and tangents
  • Step-by-step modeling: Teacher demonstration of limit evaluation
  • Interactive activities: "Approach the limit" game with function values
  • Technology integration: Use graphing calculators to visualize secants becoming tangents
  • Historical context: Discuss contributions of Newton, Leibniz, and Cauchy
  • Concept mapping: Create flowcharts connecting limits, continuity, and derivatives
Sample Activity: Calculate instantaneous velocity at t=2 for a falling object
Given: Distance s = 4.9t² meters
Solution: v = \(\lim_{h \to 0} \frac{4.9(2+h)^2 - 4.9(2)^2}{h}\)
= \(\lim_{h \to 0} \frac{4.9(4 + 4h + h^2 - 4)}{h}\) = \(\lim_{h \to 0} (19.6 + 4.9h)\) = 19.6 m/s
