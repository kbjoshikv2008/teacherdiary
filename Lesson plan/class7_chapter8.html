Content and Concepts
विषयवस्तु एवं संकल्पनाएँ
- Multiplication of fractions (whole number × fraction, fraction × whole number, fraction × fraction)
- Division of fractions (whole number ÷ fraction, fraction ÷ fraction)
- Brahmagupta's formula for fraction multiplication and division
- Fraction multiplication using area models
- Simplifying fractions to lowest form
- Relationship between products and factors
- Real-life applications of fractions
- Historical context of fractions in Indian mathematics
Learning Outcomes (NCERT)
अधिगम उपलब्धियाँ
- Students will multiply fractions using visual representations and formulas
- Students will divide fractions using reciprocal concepts
- Students will apply fraction operations to solve real-world problems
- Students will simplify fractions to lowest terms
- Students will analyze the relationship between products and factors
- Students will appreciate the historical development of fraction operations
Pedagogical Strategies
शैक्षणिक रणनीतियाँ
- Visual demonstrations with unit squares and area models
- Step-by-step fraction multiplication using Brahmagupta's formula
- Group activities solving real-life fraction problems
- Think-pair-share for division of fractions
- Historical stories about Indian mathematicians
- Fraction manipulatives and visual aids
- Error analysis of common fraction mistakes

Objectives
उद्देश्य
1. Multiply fractions using visual models and formulas
2. Divide fractions using reciprocal concepts
3. Simplify fractions to their lowest terms
4. Solve real-world problems involving fractions
5. Analyze relationships between products and factors
6. Appreciate historical contributions to fraction operations
Teaching Methodology
शिक्षण पद्धति
Period 1: Introduction to fraction multiplication (whole × fraction)
Period 2: Multiplication of fractions (fraction × fraction) using area models
Period 3: Division of fractions and reciprocal concepts
Period 4: Solving real-life problems with fraction operations
Period 5: Simplifying fractions and relationship between products/factors
Period 6: Historical context and comprehensive practice
Throughout: Hands-on activities and group problem-solving
