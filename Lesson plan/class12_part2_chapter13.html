Objectives | उद्देश्य

By the end of this chapter, students will be able to:

  • Define and calculate conditional probabilities
  • Apply multiplication theorem to dependent events
  • Differentiate between independent and mutually exclusive events
  • Use total probability theorem and Bayes' theorem
  • Define random variables and their distributions
  • Solve problems using binomial distribution
  • Calculate expected value and variance

Teaching Methodology | शिक्षण पद्धति

Periods 1-2: Introduction to Conditional Probability

  • Starter: Two-child problem and Monty Hall paradox
  • Concept: Definition of conditional probability
  • Activity: Card experiments to demonstrate P(A|B)
  • Visualization: Venn diagrams for conditional probability

Periods 3-4: Multiplication Theorem

  • Demonstration: Probability of sequential events
  • Problem Solving: Drawing balls from urns with/without replacement
  • Activity: Tree diagram construction for multi-stage experiments

Periods 5-6: Independent Events

  • Concept: Mathematical definition of independence
  • Case Study: Differentiating independent vs mutually exclusive events
  • Worksheet: Testing independence for various event pairs

Periods 7-8: Total Probability & Bayes' Theorem

  • Real-world Application: Medical diagnosis case study
  • Problem Solving: Bayes' theorem for reverse probabilities
  • Activity: Simulating Bayesian updating with dice experiments

Periods 9-10: Random Variables & Distributions

  • Concept: Discrete vs continuous random variables
  • Demonstration: Probability mass functions with examples
  • Activity: Building probability distributions for dice games

Periods 11-12: Binomial Distribution & Applications

  • Practical: Binomial experiments with coin tosses
  • Problem Solving: Quality control and defect probability
  • Assessment: Chapter review and problem-solving session
